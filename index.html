Her name is

Ayesha•

I met her somewhere between a scroll and a pause, and somehow she stayed.

I met her somewhere between
a scroll and a pause…
and somehow,
she stayed longer than she should have.

Brown hair,
soft like evening light,
eyes that don’t even try—
but still manage to hold me.

And that tiny mole near her nose…
I don’t know why,
but I noticed it
like it was meant for me to.

I thought she was bold,
the kind who doesn’t care about anything…
but I was wrong.

She’s softer than that.
Innocent in ways she probably doesn’t even realize.
And that childish side of hers…
yeah,
that’s the one that got me.

I don’t know her completely—
and maybe that’s the problem.

Because now I want to.
Slowly… properly…
like reading a book
I don’t want to finish too fast.

And the day I saw her in a saree…
I swear,
I paused for a second.

Not just because she looked beautiful—
but because she felt different.

And that tiny mole on her shoulder,
just near her collarbone…
yeah,
I noticed that too.

Like that Tamil line—
“நீ கட்டும் சேல மடிப்புல
நா கசங்கி போனேன்டி…
உன் எலுமிச்சம் பழ நிற இடுப்புல
கெறங்கி போனேன்டி…”

Because honestly,
that’s exactly what she did to me.

Ayesha,

you weren’t supposed to matter this much…
but here I am,

wanting to know you more,
a little more than I should,
like I wish to read you slowly,
feel you in little moments,
and get lost in you—
without ever wanting to find my way back.

You were supposed to be just a pause. Somehow, you became the part I keep returning to.
