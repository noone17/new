Part 2

I met her long before
this moment ever made sense—
not in passing,
not in a scroll,
but in time…
a whole year of it.

And still,
we've never stood in the same place,
never shared the same air,
never let silence sit between us in person.

Her name is Saju.

Strange, isn't it?
How someone can feel close
without ever being close enough.

I know her voice in fragments,
her thoughts in pieces,
her laughter through distance—
and yet,
it never feels incomplete.

Maybe because
some people don't arrive all at once—
they grow on you,
slowly… quietly…
until one day,
they're already important.

Saju,

I don't want a grand story,
or a perfect moment written in films—
just something real.

An evening, maybe…
by the beach,
where the sky is unsure of its colors,
and the waves keep secrets better than we do.

I'd walk beside you,
not saying too much at first—
just enough to feel the moment settle.

And maybe,
if the world pauses the right way,
I'd hold your hand…
gently, like it matters.

Maybe even pull you closer—
my hand around your waist,
not to rush anything,
just to feel
that you're finally real.

Funny thing is—
you like horror movies.

And I wonder…
if we ever sit side by side watching one,
would you get scared?

Or would I be the one
finding reasons
to stay a little closer to you.

Because after all this time,
after a whole year of knowing you—
I don't just want to know you more.

I want to meet you,
not as a thought,
not as a message,
but as a moment
I don't have to imagine anymore.

— Your Loved One
